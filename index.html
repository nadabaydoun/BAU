Water Testing Services

Subsections:

  • Drinking Water Quality Testing
  • Wastewater and Effluent Analysis
  • Surface and Groundwater Monitoring
    •

EACU provides comprehensive testing of potable, industrial, and environmental water samples. We follow national (LIBNOR) and international standards to ensure safety and compliance of drinking water.

Common tests include:

  • Physicochemical tests: pH, EC, TDS, turbidity, hardness (Total, Calcium, Magnesium), alkalinity (Total, Carbonate, Bicarbonate), color, odor
  • Heavy metals (Na, K, Pb, Cd, As, Cr, Fe, Zn, Cu, Mn, etc.) (AAS, Flame Photometer)
  • Nutrients (Nitrate NO₃⁻, Nitrite NO₂⁻, Ammonia NH₃, Phosphate PO₄³⁻)(UV‑Vis, IC)
  • Microbiological indicators (Total Coliforms, E. coli)
  • BOD₅ (5‑day incubation), COD (UV‑Vis), DO (for wastewater)
  • Inorganic ions: Chloride, Sulfate, Nitrate, Nitrite, Phosphate (Ion Chromatography, Titration)
  • Organic pollutants: VOCs, phenolics, pesticide residues, etc (GC‑MS)
  • Suspended solids: Total solids (TS), TSS
  • Sediment composition and thermal stability (TGA)

Instrumental Analysis Services

Subsections:

  • Advanced Material and Chemical Characterization
  • Elemental and Spectroscopic Analysis
  • Chromatographic Analysis
    •

EACU offers a wide range of advanced analytical instrumentation services for precise identification, quantification, and characterization of complex environmental and industrial samples. These services support academic research, quality assurance, contamination assessment, and regulatory compliance.

Available techniques include

  • GC-MS (Gas Chromatography–Mass Spectrometry):

    For detecting and identifying volatile organic compounds (VOCs), pesticides, hydrocarbons, and residual solvents.

  • HPLC (High-Performance Liquid Chromatography):

    Ideal for analyzing pharmaceuticals, organic acids, preservatives, dyes, and water-soluble compounds.

  • IR (Infrared Spectroscopy / FTIR):

    For functional group identification in organic and polymeric compounds, including material fingerprinting.

  • TGA (Thermogravimetric Analysis):

    For studying thermal stability, decomposition behavior, and moisture/volatile content in solids and polymers.

  • Elemental Analysis:

    Accurate quantification of carbon, hydrogen, nitrogen, and sulfur (CHNS) in environmental or food samples.

  • IC (Ion Chromatography):

    For analyzing anions and cations in water, wastewater, and other environmental matrices (e.g., nitrate, sulfate, chloride, ammonium,…).

  • AAS (Atomic Absorption Spectroscopy):

    Precise detection of heavy metals such as Pb, Cd, Cr, Zn, Fe and others.

  • UV-Vis Spectrophotometry:

    Routine analysis of nutrients, color, and other chemical species.

  • Kjeldahl Nitrogen Analysis:

    Determination of total nitrogen in organic and food samples.

Available Techniques

GC-MS

Gas Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry Combines gas chromatography and mass spectrometry to identify substances in a sample.
  • Pesticide residue analysis in food and water
  • Volatile organic compounds (VOCs)
  • Environmental pollutants
  • Pharmaceuticals

HPLC

High-Performance Liquid Chromatography A separation technique for identifying, quantifying, and purifying individual components in a mixture.
  • Detection of preservatives and additives in food
  • Drug analysis
  • Mycotoxin detection
  • Organic acid analysis in fertilizers

AAS

Atomic Absorption Spectrophotometer Measures the concentration of metal ions in samples.
  • Heavy metals in water and food (Pb, Cd, Cr, etc.)
  • Trace elements in fertilizers and soil
  • Toxic metal residues in wastewater

Ion Chromatography

IC Separation and analysis of ions and polar molecules.
  • Anions and cations in water: nitrate, nitrite, chloride, sulfate, phosphate
  • Analysis of inorganic nutrients in fertilizers

UV-Vis Spectrophotometer

Uses ultraviolet and visible light to analyze absorbance.
  • Determination of BOD, COD, ammonia, phosphate
  • Color intensity in food and water
  • Quantification of pigments, dyes, and phenols

Elemental Analyzer

Determines the elemental composition (C, H, N, S, O) in organic and inorganic materials.
  • Organic matter in soil and fertilizers
  • Protein and carbon content in food
  • Total nitrogen for Kjeldahl comparisons

Kjeldahl Nitrogen Analyzer

Measures nitrogen content via digestion and titration.
  • Total nitrogen in fertilizers
  • Protein estimation in food
  • Soil fertility analysis

Flame Photometer

Measures concentration of alkali and alkaline earth metals.
  • Sodium, potassium, calcium analysis in water and fertilizers
  • Salinity checks

TGA

Thermogravimetric Analyzer Measures weight changes in materials as a function of temperature.
  • Moisture and volatile matter in fertilizers
  • Thermal stability of materials
  • Organic content in solids

FTIR

Fourier Transform Infrared Spectroscopy Identifies organic and some inorganic compounds based on IR absorption.
  • Functional group identification in complex samples
  • Polymer and contaminant identification
  • Structural analysis of agricultural inputs

Sample Submission & Forms

We’re here to make your sample submission process as smooth and clear as possible. To ensure accurate testing and valid results, please follow the steps below when preparing and submitting your samples to the Environmental Analysis and Consultancy Unit (EACU).

  Step 1: Download & Complete the Right Form

Before submitting your samples, make sure you download and complete the appropriate submission form that matches your needs:


  • Water Sample Submission Form
  • Food Sample Submission Form
  • Fertilizer & Pesticide Testing Request Form

Each form includes fields for your contact information, details about the samples, and the tests you’d like us to perform.

  Step 2: Prepare Your Samples Carefully

To ensure accurate and reliable results:

  • Label each sample clearly with:
    • Sample ID
    • Date & time of collection
    • Location/site
    • Collector's name
  • Use clean containers and avoid cross-contamination:
    • Rinse containers with the sample unless preservatives are pre-added
    • Fill containers properly (e.g., leave headspace for BOD tests)
    • Wear gloves when handling samples

  Step 3: Preserve Samples Correctly

Proper preservation helps maintain the integrity of your samples:

  • For metals in water: Acidify with HNO₃ to pH < 2
  • For nutrients: Use H₂SO₄ as required
  • For microbiological and general chemistry tests: Keep samples chilled at or below 6 °C
  • Do not freeze samples unless specifically instructed

Please use preservatives approved or provided by our unit.

  Step 4: Transport Safely

  • Keep samples in a cooler with ice packs if they need to stay cold
  • Avoid delays - timing matters!
  • Make sure containers are sealed securely
  • Always include the completed submission form and maintain the chain-of-custody with the samples throughout transport

  Step 5: Final Checks before Submission

  • Submit samples within holding time limits, such as:
    • Microbiological tests: within 6–24 hours
    • Nutrients (if preserved): within 28 days
    • Metals (if preserved): up to 6 months
  • Make sure your chain-of-custody form is signed by everyone who handled the samples
